It is a beautiful display of hospitality and commitment to the body of Christ when [[accommodations]] are made for those with disabilities, especially between lay people and clergy. 

---

**Original Highlight:**

“Other participants commented that whenever an individual church receives a new clergy member, traditions and practices are adapted to that particular person’s way of doing things. Participants felt that making adaptations for a clergy with a disability would generally be the same.” ([Brennan and Rutledge, 2013, p. 84](zotero://select/groups/5230851/items/WE6W54QM))
