Thankfully in the early 2000s there was evidence of education exploring [[sexuality]], [[gender roles]], and [[women in ministry]], but almost nothing for those with [[disability]].

**Original Highlight:**

<mark style="background-color: #a28ae5">Quote</mark> <br>Similarly, between 1999-2000, [[Taura-Jean Gilbert]] studied fourteen United Church of Christ seminaries, using a combination of interviews (faculty, students, administrators, alumni), a survey, document analysis, and site visits to learn about acceptance and [[accommodation]] of students with [[disability]]. She found that [[seminaries]] were making progress regarding physical [[accessibility]], but that little was being accomplished in terms of [[curriculum]], even while schools typically had courses on women, [[gender roles]], and [[sexualities]].[^17]
