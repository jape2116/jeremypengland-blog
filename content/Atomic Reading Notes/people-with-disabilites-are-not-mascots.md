Are people with [[disability]] viewed as special because they are uniquely special, or it because because they are propped up as a mascot?

---

**Original Highlight:**

“However, clergy with disabilities were generally conceived of as being special, more compassionate, better listeners, and more able to be empathetic. Their disability gave them insight into the human condition. This belief of “specialness” is sometimes typical of able-bodied persons’ attitudes toward PWD—that they are unique and often portrayed in society as heroes, having overcome many personal and societal obstacles.” ([Brennan and Rutledge, 2013, p. 87](zotero://select/groups/5230851/items/WE6W54QM))
