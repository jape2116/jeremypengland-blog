- St. Moses the Black
	- changed from living a life of robbery to becoming one of the desert fathers.
	- He was a slave who became a bandit. He robbed and did plenty of other things. One day, when he was on the run, he hid in a monastery. He liked what he saw there, so he asked the abbot if he could stay. The abbot was naturally reluctant, since this was a man with a violent and dangerous record, but he gave the guy a chance. He struggled with a violent temper, but he became a monk renowned for his wisdom. Once, he was robbed by men from his old gang, but since he was the strongest of them, he overpowered them and brought them to the monastery for his brothers to decide what to do with them. They became monks as well because.
	- Eventually, he grew old. His monastery was raided. He stayed behind in order to distract the raiders while the rest escaped and was killed.
