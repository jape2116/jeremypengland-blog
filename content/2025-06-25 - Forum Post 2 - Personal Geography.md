 Personal geography is an interesting question because I can point to and remember every placed I live, it really can be difficult to remember so many of the details until they are pried from my brain, sometimes in unique and surprising ways. 
 
 Below I have a list of the places I live, and I had originally started to write about each place, but realized how long that would have been just with short summaries. I felt a sense of guilt that I couldn't honor each place appropriately. I found that to be a weird feeling to have because with so many places it would seem that there would not be enough time to make them meaningful. However, as I reflected more about that feeling I think I've come to the realization that with so much movement in my life, each of those places serve as anchor points and chapters in my life, and they deserve to be honored as important markers of my Journey.

Those anchor points actually serve as pilgrimages stops (or shrines) because they mark occasions of formation and change. If I'm ever in the areas of any of my past homes, I at least like to drive by them and allow memories to come back. 

I do want to highlight a few interesting parts of this journey though, starting with Orlando. I was born there and lived there until I was two years old. I have no memory, but I do feel a sense of belonging when I visit. It's my place of origin so to speak, so it will be special to me. 

Fremont, OH is kind of a central hub for my family. It's a small town surrounded by farms. It's where I learned to love fairs and demolition derbies. However, it is the town in which my parents divorced.

Toledo is the city I spent many formative years of my life. I lived on the "bad" side of town and the good side of town. We always rented and had many neighbors. It's where I learned to be in close quarters with others and not really mind it all. 

After high school graduation, I went to basic training and advanced training, and while there my mom, brother, and sister moved, meaning I had no home any longer. I moved in with my Dad near Cleveland for a year before college. 

When I started at Bowling Green State University, I found a real sense of home for the first time. I was relatively alone in terms of family support, but I didn't mind it at all. I made a ton of friends, found my hobbies, and had a real sense of purpose. During that time I deployed twice: once to Kuwait (hot, humid, and rich) and Iraq (south was desert and north was green), and once to Afghanistan (a beautiful country). In Afghanistan I experienced loss and trauma. After both deployments though, I came "home" to BGSU. 

When I graduated, my now wife and I moved to Lima, OH so I could teach. A smaller city than Toledo, but urban nonetheless. Here we discovered the Nazarene church. After a year we moved to Monroe, MI with my wife's parents to save up for a house. I was teaching music online and went to lead worship at a Nazarene church, which I eventually ended up pastoring. 

The beautiful coincidence of that small church was it was on the East Side of Toledo, a mile from my old house, and 2 miles from my old elementary school, which was next to a park where I got to plant a tree as a 2nd grader. Then we moved from Monroe, MI to a house on the West Side of Toledo, which was 2 miles from my old apartment and 2 miles from my high school. In a unique way that I don't quite understand yet, I was placed in the sphere of two previous location. 

After I felt called away, we looked for a new church in a large city, and we got a call to pastor here in Garland, TX, a city of 300,000 people, and is a suburb to Dallas. It's now been 3.5 years since I've been here and I am learning to be settled in the space, because my natural routine has been to spend about 3 years in a location before moving. I have to learn to be content in this space, and with this class, have learned about pilgrimage. When I get to visit these places, they are checkpoints of my life that have led me here.
