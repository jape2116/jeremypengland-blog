# Behold the Lamb of God by Andrew Peterson

Overall, I think this album highlights the complexity and intimacy we experience as individuals in the contexts we're in. From striving for the desire of someone to lead us, to the chaos that is giving birth, and finally the realities of trusting in Jesus amidst a broken world. Humans have always been a part of the story, just not the saviors of the story. 

Top 3 tracks in order after first listen through: 
1. So Long, Moses
2. Labor of Love
3. Behold the Lamb of God

## Thoughts of each track

1. I wonder if the disciples knew the prophecies of the coming Jesus when they decided to follow him. Like I wonder if it was like Harry Potter where everybody knew who Harry Potter was and were super excited to get to know him. His first song reminds me of how we can just jump straight to the exciting bits And talk about the prophecies for told, but kind of discounting the whole of Jesus's life. And it's interesting that Jesus's birth was both foretold and celebrated by outside influences. But the new life through death wasn't necessarily accepted when proclaimed by Jesus himself.

2. A song of history. Leans more into a penal substitution theology of salvation. I like how it ties the story of Passover in with the blood of Jesus (though in the song the judgement of death passes and then mercy....but in my literal mind it seems like it would be too late.....you'd already be dead 😂) 

3. So far, top song. Just musically is so good. It really highlights the hope we find in the next human leader. Highlights the desire to be subjegated so many people have (even Americans in today's context). But then the middle of the song changes composition. It's familiar, but also slightly different, right when they start talking about the coming of Jesus. (Goosebumps around 4:30) This may be because we call Jesus king, but he is radically different than the other kings we know. It lives much more in the tension than the other songs to me. Reminds me of some lyrics from a Styx song called "Fallen Angel" which starts out: 
> Hell nobody's perfect
> One hundred percent 
> No saint, no Pope, no King no Queen
> No President
> 
> But our hunger for heroes
> Has made us blind
> We seek salvation
> From the cup of human kind
> 
> But every time we hear the voice
> Of some new Abraham
> We wake too late to realize
> It was just another scam

4. This songs seems like the prayer one might respond to when thinking about the people in the 2 Chronicles passage from 10/27. "We are bound, so we turn to you." Beautiful prayer song.
5. Beautiful instrumental. In an odd time signature, which creates a much human element to a pray like this.
6. This man is a genius for being able to put the whole lineage into a catchy song 😆. However, what a great way to show the lineage of Jesus. Highlights legacy and faith that extends into the generations that follow. 
7. I like this song because it is focused more on the struggle a faithful person like Joseph may face when following the ways of God. There might not always be a "good" feeling decision, but following God is good. Also, the idea that supporting characters are important to the story. Joseph, I would argue, probably isn't thought of as a main character. Sure we mention him in all of the Bible stories, but mostly because he's there. However, I'm sure to Mary he was the most important of men in her life. He was a main character to her, which allows the blessing of Jesus to be able to be born.
8. This song made me tear up. Kind of crazy to know that something as intimate as Mary's sex life is known for all of history, and the squalor of the location in which she gives birth is highlighted as "prophecy of fulfilled." I recall the birth of our kids at home, and how many emotions and feelings came along with that. It was intimate, scary, awe-inspiring, and miraculous all at once. Mary and Joseph go through that. Highlight of this song is that we talk about real people with real experiences we can relate with. Beautiful song.
9. I like how both well known hymns so far have been instrumental. We want to sing along because they are comfortable, but it's ok not too and enjoy them in a different format.
10. Proclamation song! I would be tempted to replace Joy to the World (not even a Christmas song anyways) with this song. 
11. This reminds me of a song that is played over a montage of sorts in a movie, perhaps the coming of the wise men bringing their gifts. But what I think it highlights is a wonderment. We just came out of a proclamation song, but wonder is different. Wonder doesn't always have to lead shouting, it can lead to contemplation, silence, questioning, awe, etc. 
12. A song of progression through a spiritual moment. Trust in Jesus but also dealing with doubts and brokenness, a remembrance of who Jesus is fully, and then clear and distinct proclamation. Again, another highlight of the complexity of the human experience that, if rooted in Jesus, will lead to breakthroughs of complete trust and excitement for the life we have in him.
